PROFESSIONAL BIO:

I was introduced to technology and the medical field through the medical field. Early in my 20's I helped a behavioral health private practice revamp all their computer systems, modernize their billing and collection software, and transition them from an entirely paper based charting system to an Electronic Medical Record. After attending college for a few semesters, I felt like I was missing something in my life though, I hadn't really found anything that fascinated me, until I became an EMT.


I was an EMT in both the deserts of Moab as well as in the Emergency Room of the University of Utah Hospital. Some key accomplishments that I obtained there were:

  • Member of the Cardiac Arrest Rapid Response Team
  • EMT Preceptor and Critical Care Skills Trainer
  • Member of the Trauma Team
  • Member of the Critical Care Team
  • Medical Skill Preceptor to Resident Doctors for various skills within my scope

I was exposed to many Electronic Medical Record Systems such as Epic and InSync, Medical Image Sharing Databases such as PACS, and many other cutting edge tools such as Ultrasound Guided Procedures and Cardiac Monitoring software. Over the course of the last 5 years as medicine has captivated my mind, I have learned my true passion lies with the technology side of things and creating the tools of tomorrow. My vision is to write code that saves lives

Eir Scheduling Solutions:

This project is a Full-Stack application. The server has API's that the front end can use to obtain the data that is populated on the calendar. The software is a simple scheduling application geared towards doctors offices. with it you can:

  • Securely Register and login
  • Create a New Patient
  • Create New Appointments that are linked to patients on the server
  • All Calendar events are clickable and draggable
  • Data persists and is stored securely in a database
